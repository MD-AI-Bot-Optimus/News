3.1. The Core Argument: "Internal" vs. "External"

The document highlights that selling properties to outsiders is "catastrophic" for Estate wealth due to Leakage (money leaving the Estate).

  • Internal Settlement: The Estate voluntarily "stresses" (discounts) the value by 20% to make buying each other out easier. No actual tax is paid to the government; the value stays within the brothers.
  • Market Sale: You lose money twice—first to a Market Discount (buyers bargaining you down) and second to a tax drag proxy (illustrative) (a simplified estimate for negotiation; not a literal tax calculation).

    4.1 The "Acquirer-Pays" Principle

    When the 505+506 block is split, the son choosing the individual unit (506) is responsible for the financial "leakage" caused by that decision. This ensures the other brothers are not penalized for the loss of the combined asset's premium.

    4.2 Breakdown of Penalties (The "Break-Up Cost")

    • 1. Physical Remediation (Kitchen): ₹0.30 cr
      Reason: Flat 506 must be made a self-contained unit by adding/separating walls and plumbing from 505.
    • 2. Forced Sale Leakage on 505: ₹0.6375 cr
      Reason: By taking 506, the son forces the estate to sell 505 as a standalone studio under Forced Sale conditions.
      Calculation: Market Value of 505 (₹1.70 cr) × 37.5% (Market discount + tax drag proxy).
    • 3. Total Separation Penalty: ₹0.9375 cr
