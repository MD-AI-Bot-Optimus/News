Scenario 4 Baseline | Estate Settlement

Earmark-only uplift: 50% of Arshee to Son3, remainder split 1/3 each (including remaining 50% Arshee), with Model-B loan protection for Son2. Dec 2025

0. Executive Summary

This document provides a clean “Estate Pool + Ledger” settlement model for a single baseline rule-set: Scenario 4 (baseline) where only 50% of Arshee is earmarked to Son3 (elder-care uplift), and all remaining estate value is split equally (1/3 each) — including the remaining 50% of Arshee. Son2’s loan protection is preserved using Model B.

What this changes vs the Elder-care uplift file

  • Uplift is only the earmark of 50% of Arshee to Son3.
  • The remaining 50% of Arshee is treated as part of the shared pool (1/3 each), not pre-split 25/25/50.
  • Loan to Son2 is preserved via Model B: Son1 and Son3 each fund half of the credit.
  • Escrow is always an estate-level holdback (not paid by any individual son).

1. Locked Parameters

1.1 Model Structure

  • Estate Pool + Ledger: Each scenario assigns each property to exactly one son, then uses ledger equalisation cash to reach entitlements.
  • Scenario 4 baseline uplift: Earmark 50% of Arshee to Son3; split the remaining pool 1/3 each.
  • Loan protection (Model B): Son1 −₹0.2675cr, Son2 +₹0.5350cr, Son3 −₹0.2675cr.
  • Escrow: ₹0.05cr held at estate level before entitlements (no individual payer).

2. Fixed Inputs

2.1 Estate Pool (Stressed / Internal)

  • Horizon (505+506): ₹3.96cr
  • Arshee: ₹3.40cr
  • Natasha (estate 50% only): ₹0.60cr
  • Total Estate Pool: ₹7.96cr

2.2 Escrow & Loan

  • Escrow: ₹0.05cr (estate-level holdback)
  • Loan owed to Son2: ₹0.5350cr (protected via Model B adjustment)

Baseline Entitlement Math (Scenario 4 Baseline)

  • Step A (Earmark uplift): 50% of Arshee → Son3 = 0.50 × 3.40 = ₹1.70cr
  • Step B (Remaining pool): Total 7.96 − 1.70 = ₹6.26cr
  • Step C (Equal split of remaining pool): 6.26 ÷ 3 = ₹2.0867cr each (rounded)
  • Pre-loan subtotals: Son1 = 2.0867; Son2 = 2.0867; Son3 = 2.0867 + 1.70 = 3.7867
  • Loan adjustment (Model B): Son1: −0.2675; Son2: +0.5350; Son3: −0.2675
  • Final entitlements (rounded to 4dp): Son1 = ₹1.8192cr; Son2 = ₹2.6217cr; Son3 = ₹3.5192cr

Note: Rounding is displayed at 4 decimals. Totals may show a ~₹0.0001cr rounding difference.

Scenario 3: Forced Sale

Sale Economics (reference vs gross ₹9.95cr)

  • Discount: 30% → ₹2.985cr
  • Net after discount: ₹6.965cr
  • CGT @10%: ₹0.6965cr
  • Post-tax proceeds: ₹6.2685cr (≈ 40% loss vs gross)

Escrow and loan are estate-level governance items and not “market loss.” Forced sale destroys value via sequential leakage (discount then tax).

Scenario 4: Partial Sale

Example: Sell Natasha (estate 50%) only

  • Reference value: ₹0.75cr (undiscounted)
  • Soft sale discount: 10% → ₹0.675cr
  • CGT @10% on discounted price: ₹0.0675cr
  • Post-tax proceeds: ₹0.6075cr vs internal stressed retention ₹0.60cr

Partial sales typically incur discount and tax leakage; internal retention keeps governance simple and avoids fragmentation risk.

7. Advisory Guidance

Neutral, audit-grade guidance to maximize family value under the Scenario 4 baseline (50% Arshee earmark to Son3, remainder split equally), with Model-B loan protection for Son2 and estate-level escrow governance.

Family-level guidance

  • Keep 505+506 combined: Combined horizon preserves value. Breaking up introduces a mandatory kitchen remediation (₹0.30 cr) and an additional 10% fire-sale penalty on the post-stress value if separately marketed. These costs are borne by the party causing separation, not the estate.
  • Avoid forced/discounted sales: Sale leakage is sequential — price discount followed by tax. Internal settlement using stress on asset values avoids discount+tax leakage and keeps governance simple.
  • Eliminate co-ownership early: Convert partials to single-owner holdings (e.g., Natasha) via a clean buyout of the estate-owned 50% to avoid minority-holder disadvantage and litigation risk.
  • Respect adjacency constraints: Son1 cannot live next to Son2 or Son3 (505 vs 506). Any allocation violating this is infeasible.
  • Escrow is estate-level: Fund ₹0.05 cr from the gross estate before distribution; hold in neutral escrow (lawyer/notary). Any unused amount can be redistributed per scenario split logic.
  • Loan optics: Son2’s loan creates no upside; it is credited via Model B into entitlements (Son2 +₹0.535 cr; Son1 −₹0.2675 cr; Son3 −₹0.2675 cr). Do not treat the loan as property or subject to split.

Guidance for Son1

  • Target single-owner outcomes: Prefer owning Natasha outright (post buyout of estate 50%) or holding 505+506 as a single block with clear equalisation payments you will owe/receive per scenario math.
  • Use rental cash-flow payouts when Arshee is with Son3: If you have a shortfall vs entitlement, prefer fixed monthly payouts from Arshee’s net surplus (~₹80,000/month, 0% interest) instead of forced asset sales. This provides predictable liquidity and preserves family value.
  • Avoid 505/506 separation: Do not trigger the kitchen remediation or fire-sale discount; these are direct capital losses to you if you cause separation.
  • Document equalisation: Agree a simple ledger schedule (amounts, dates, bank route). Include an escrow reference and a dispute-resolution clause (neutral notary/arbitration).

Guidance for Son2

  • Loan credit is fixed: You receive ₹0.535 cr via Model B in entitlements; do not expect any profit or interest. The loan is not split and does not carry upside.
  • Match funding to equalisation: If you owe equalisation or need to acquire property (e.g., Natasha 50% or Horizon), plan immediate liquidity against the schedule; avoid forced sales to raise cash.
  • Prefer clean allocations: If you take Horizon, settle equalisation to Son1/Son3 promptly. If you take Natasha (estate 50%), keep documentation clear that buyout relates only to the estate-owned share.
  • Governance hygiene: Keep written records for all transfers; route via bank; anchor amounts to the entitlement math in this document.

Guidance for Son3

  • Arshee as long-term hold: Retaining Arshee while paying Son1 via rental cash-flow is the dominant structure for value preservation and liquidity predictability.
  • Pay fixed installments: Use a simple schedule (monthly ₹80,000, 0% interest) until Son1’s shortfall is cleared; include annual reconciliation and an optional prepayment clause.
  • Avoid fragmentation: Do not separate 505/506; if you acquire Horizon, pay equalisation promptly to eliminate co-ownership optics and minority-holder risk.
  • Keep uplift rule intact: Under this baseline, 50% of Arshee is earmarked to you; maintain clarity that the remaining pool is split equally and that loan credits are handled via Model B.

Process & documentation

  • Open escrow: Create the ₹0.05 cr escrow with a neutral lawyer/notary. Define release conditions and redistribution rules for unused amounts.
  • Freeze values: Reference the stressed estate values used in this file for internal settlement; avoid mixing sale-side discount+tax math into settlement.
  • Execute a term sheet: One-page ledger with property allocations, entitlements, equalisation flows, dates, bank details, and adjacency compliance.
  • Record and notarize: Register transfers/buyouts; store digital and physical copies; maintain an audit trail.
  • Dispute resolution: Include a neutral arbitration clause to avoid litigation; define timelines and escalation steps.

30 / 60 / 90 day activation checklist

  • Day 0–30: Agree allocations; open escrow; sign the ledger term sheet; schedule equalisation payments.
  • Day 31–60: Execute property transfers/buyouts (e.g., Natasha 50%); start rental payout schedule if applicable.
  • Day 61–90: Complete equalisation flows; notarize final ownership; close or redistribute unused escrow; archive documents.

Hard rules (do not override)

  • Do not merge sale math into settlement math.
  • Do not double-count Natasha (only estate-owned 50% is included).
  • Do not add interest unless explicitly specified (rental payout uses 0%).
  • Respect adjacency constraints for 505/506.
  • Break-up penalties (₹0.30 cr remediation + 10% post-stress fire-sale discount) are borne by the party causing separation, not the estate.

Minimal success criteria

  • All entitlements met per this document’s math.
  • Each property has exactly one owner; co-ownership eliminated.
  • Escrow funded and governed neutrally.
  • Loan credited via Model B; no upside, no split.
  • Equalisation payments completed on schedule; audit trail preserved.
